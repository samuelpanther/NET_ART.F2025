YouTube, 2022

Soda stitches together a performance of women's apologies compiled from all over YouTube over the course of several years. The apologies, mostly focusing on the mundane and trivial such as outside noises, poor quality videos, untidy spaces, imperfect eyebrows, makeup, hair and so on, merge and then morph into a work that takes on a sinister life of its own, trite, full of sarcasm and insecurity, among other things .

Are these apologies an overt part of video performance, or are they subconscious? In an irl world where women constantly, reflexively apologize on a daily basis for not being or 'performing' up to an expected standard, social media magnifies the urgency of viewer expectation.

"My Apology" pinpoints the paradoxical insincerity and self-deprecation involved in this specific form of emotional labor, critiquing it while drawing attention to these expectations.

Soda allows viewers to further judge for themselves by providing two additional layers of the work in the form of ordered reference lists. The accompanying Google document includes transcriptions of each apology along with links to the original YouTube videos, and the YouTube Playlist provides the viewer a seamless reference experience of watching the original apologies.
