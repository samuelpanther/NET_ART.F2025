YouTube, 2020

Soda flips the visual script of niche social media make-up+life-hack+positivity videos. The viewer sees only the artist's hands, covered in makeup, rooting through a beat-up, plastic bag of old, broken, leaky makeup items, picking them up and showing them off, smearing makeup on her hands, all as life guidance and positivity platitudes show on the screen. This work pits the societal veneer of self-improvement often equated with toxic positivity, and the expecations it carries, with reality that it's not so easy to keep everything sparkly and clean—as many a makeup bag or drawer in the world can attest, resulting in a satirical piece that luxuriates in mess and reality.
